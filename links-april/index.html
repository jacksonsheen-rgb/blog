Links I've Been Sitting With

A handful of things from the internet that stuck around longer than a tab.

This week's collection

Sometimes you open a link and close it. Sometimes it stays open for days. These are the second kind.

On craft: There's a piece about Japanese joinery that gets at something I've been thinking about — the idea that the strongest connections are the ones you can't see.

On music: Somebody described Andre 3000's flute album as "what happens when you stop performing and start playing." That landed.

On cities: The best neighborhoods aren't designed. They accumulate. They're the result of a thousand small decisions made by people who actually live there.

The city is not a problem to be solved. It is a process to be experienced.

Why I collect these

Links are bookmarks for your brain. Most of them expire. But the ones that don't — those are telling you something about what you care about right now.

Pay attention to what you can't close.
