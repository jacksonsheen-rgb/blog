## The palette of memory

There's a reason certain colors make you feel something before you can name it. Green is the easiest color for the human eye to process. Brown is the color of earth, wood, leather — things that age well.

Together they form something warm without being loud. Grounded without being heavy.

## Frank's greens

If you listen to *Blonde* with your eyes closed, you might see green. Not neon, not forest — something in between. The green of a hotel room at 3am with one lamp on. The green of a pool at dusk.

> I'm sure we're taller in another dimension.

Color isn't just what you see. It's what you feel when you see it. That's why design matters — not as decoration, but as **emotional architecture**.

## Applying this

When I chose the palette for this space, I wanted it to feel like:

- A library with natural light
- The inside of a vintage jacket
- A conversation that goes long past midnight

Whether it hits that mark is up to you. But the intention is there.
